A common question in data engineering interviews is: "When do you switch from Pandas to PySpark?"

### Pandas: The Single-Node King

Pandas is incredible for data manipulation. It's fast, expressive, and easy to use. However, Pandas must load the entire dataset into RAM on a single machine. If you have a 16GB RAM laptop and try to read a 20GB CSV, Pandas will crash with an OutOfMemory error.

### PySpark: The Distributed Engine

PySpark is built for distributed computing. It partitions data across hundreds of machines. You can process petabytes of data without running out of memory.

<Callout type="warning">Don't use Spark for small datasets (e.g., 100MB). The overhead of distributing tasks will make it significantly slower than Pandas.</Callout>
