Data modeling is how we structure data for analytical querying. The most famous approach is the Kimball Star Schema.

### Fact Tables

Fact tables store quantitative data (measurements/metrics). Think of them as events. Examples: `sales`, `page_views`, `rides`.

### Dimension Tables

Dimension tables store descriptive attributes related to the facts. They answer the "who, what, where, when". Examples: `users`, `products`, `locations`.

By joining a central Fact table to multiple Dimension tables, the schema looks like a star!
